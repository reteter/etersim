# Grill brief — M6 First zoom-out

A **grill brief** is the scenario for a future owner grill: the questions that grill
must resolve, the rails already locked, and the known traps. Written at the
fantasy-roadmap grill (2026-07-16). M6 is behind the credits — grill it after M5 is
specced; its **first question is architectural and hard to reverse**.

**This grill decides:** the recursion architecture, the administrator mechanic, the
second region's opening, and long inter-region crossings.

## Rails (locked, don't relitigate — link, build on)

- Arc: **contractor → discoverer → guide of the guilds' expansion** — the cartel
  follows the company it sent into the dark; delegation is the natural consequence,
  never a menu that "unlocked" (2026-07-16, lock 7).
- **Administrator is a character, not sliders**: nominated by the guild convent
  (dispatch), confirmed by the player, given a **coarse verbal mandate** ("don't
  fear risk", "grow the fleet"); the delegated region folds into **reports** — a
  tab in the spirit of the HQ company-state view, in someone's voice (lock 8).
- Delegation is progression, never forced — manual play always remains (Lens
  ladder law).
- The multiregion Price board is a *different* board — the role change is the point.
- Events gradient level 2: travel hazards on crossings (aether storms, currents) —
  still no combat (ADR-0004).
- Arcana installment 2 = **scale-up** of M4's Processing machinery: chains whose
  inputs and outputs live in different regions (Arcana split as amended 2026-07-16).
- Branch offices unlock the buildings mechanic in a new region (E9 grill hook).

## Questions the grill must resolve

1. **Recursion architecture (first, hard to reverse):** does Multiregion
   re-instantiate the region sim — a region as a node running the same abstract
   machinery? (Flagged since the farewell grill.) Related note (2026-07-16, lock 8):
   **E11's Policy is the embryo of the administrator** — a deterministic strategy
   playing a region; the mandate as Policy selection/parameterization. If the
   delegated region *is* a sim run by a Policy, recursion and delegation are one
   mechanism — evaluate together, decide once.
2. Administrator fidelity: does the delegated region simulate fully, coarsely, or
   only in summary while unobserved? What does a report contain (and hide)?
   Determinism across delegation must hold (ADR-0003).
3. Mandate vocabulary v1: which verbal policies exist, what do they map to, how
   does the player learn what their administrator actually did (trust needs
   verification — the Ledger precedent suggests an administrator ledger)?
4. Old region access: the grill must answer the flagged UX promise — the old
   region's Price board stays reachable on demand (2026-07-16 open item); at what
   cost/fidelity?
5. The second region itself: generated how (template + new seed — worldgen already
   supports it), different archetype mix? What does the player start with there
   (branch office founding = the new region's HQ moment)?
6. Crossings: how long, what hazards, what does a crossing cost — and does the
   Expedition's own voyage set the fictional precedent for the route?
7. Where exactly does 1.0 end: which M6 slice ships before credits (the PRD says
   second region opens + first administrator appears → credits) vs which is
   post-credits sandbox?

## Traps

- Deciding recursion casually: it's the one decision here that's expensive to
  reverse — it deserves the session's freshest hour, evidence from E11's harness
  (can a Policy actually play a region competently?), and possibly a prototype.
- Administrator as a chore: if reports demand reaction like a second job, the
  reward inverts — reading a trusted voice must feel like *less* load, not more.
- Second region as "the same game again": the role change (guide, different board,
  new chains) is what keeps it from being a reskin of hour one.
- Don't design Multiregion proper here: M6 is the *first* zoom-out — tariffs,
  politics, region-wide buildings stay post-1.0 (Lens ladder).
