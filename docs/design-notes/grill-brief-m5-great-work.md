# Grill brief — M5 The Great Work (the Expedition)

A **grill brief** is the scenario for a future owner grill: the questions that grill
must resolve, the rails already locked, and the known traps. Written at the
fantasy-roadmap grill (2026-07-16). This is **the big one** — budget a full session;
run it only after M4's Processing has shipped and been playtested (the funnel's
feeder must exist before the funnel is sized).

**This grill decides:** the commissioning flow, the construction machinery at Great
Work scale, contract-system integration, pacing to credits, and the staging of the
finale.

## Rails (locked, don't relitigate — link, build on)

- The Expedition is **the guilds' project; the player is the indispensable
  contractor** — rank culmination precedes it; the convocation arrives as a *Głos
  Eteru* dispatch. Two gestures belong to the player: **accepting the commission
  and choosing the Expedition shipyard's port** (2026-07-16, lock 6).
- **The funnel**: the Great Work consumes mainly **processed goods** (M4
  Processing) — building an industry is the prerequisite, piles are not enough.
- **The finale is a voyage**: the colossus departs past the outermost ring, the
  camera follows, the region shrinks astern to a node — credits; the Pale Blue Dot
  paraphrase (#237) plays *during* the zoom-out, not on a separate screen. *Głos
  Eteru* covers construction milestones (rising pulse, not just a progress bar).
- Great Work law (Lens ladder): commissioned by the level's institutions,
  generalizes the Build Order machinery; completing it opens the next level.
- Pacing anchor: 1.0 ≈ 8–12 h to credits; the Great Work phase ≤ ~⅓ of that.
- No-debt, Reserve, agency guarantee — a super-construction may slow the game,
  never kill it.

## Questions the grill must resolve

1. Commission trigger: what exactly is "rank culmination" (rank 4 in how many
   guilds? all five?) — and what does the permit ladder pay at ranks 3–4 so the
   climb keeps paying (open flank from 2026-07-16, lock 5)?
2. The commission's contract shape: one mega-contract (continuous, settlement
   periods — reusing E3 machinery) or a new Build-Order-at-scale with guild
   co-funding (fee per met milestone)? Do the guilds *pay* the player, or does the
   player fund glory (reputation as the only fee)?
3. Site mechanics: what does the chosen port gain/suffer (market impact of the
   auto-draw at scale — E9's Build Order showed a site drains its local market;
   at Great Work scale that's a regional event in itself)?
4. Recipe shape: streams over time (quota per period, contract-like) vs a grand
   total; how many goods, what raw/processed mix; one construction stage or staged
   phases (keel/hull/provisioning) each with its own dispatch headline?
5. Pacing: target world-days for the build; what does the E11 harness assert
   (reachability by a competent policy within the anchor; no-dominance of any
   single feeder strategy)?
6. Finale staging tech: the zoom-out voyage — camera, monologue text length (#237:
   which fragment of the paraphrase), what the player can still do during it, and
   what "after credits" state the save lands in (M6 opens here).
7. What happens to the region's economy when the Great Work completes — does the
   consumption vacuum snap back (a visible peace dividend)?

## Traps

- Designing the Great Work before Processing is real: the funnel's shape depends on
  chain depth that M4 will teach us — hence the sequencing rule above.
- A progress bar without a story: the milestones-by-dispatch lock exists precisely
  so the build reads as a narrative arc.
- Contract-system bypass: if the Great Work invents parallel machinery instead of
  generalizing Build Order + Contracts, M5 doubles the code and halves the theme
  (the final exam of *reliability* must feel like the contracts the player knows).
- The finale must not be missable-by-boredom: if the build's tail is flat, the
  player alt-tabs through their own crowning — pacing beats completeness.
